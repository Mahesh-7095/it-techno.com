HSL Colors

What is HSL?

HSL stands for Hue, Saturation, Lightness. It is a color model where colors are defined by:

  • Hue: A degree on the color wheel (0-360).
  • Saturation: A percentage (0% = gray, 100% = full color).
  • Lightness: A percentage (0% = black, 100% = white).

Example: hsl(0, 100%, 50%) represents pure red.

HSL Syntax

The syntax for HSL colors is:


hsl(hue, saturation, lightness)

Where hue is a degree (0-360), and saturation and lightness are percentages (0%-100%).

Examples of HSL Colors


<div style="background-color: hsl(0, 100%, 50%);"></div> <!-- Red -->
<div style="background-color: hsl(120, 100%, 50%);"></div> <!-- Green -->
<div style="background-color: hsl(240, 100%, 50%);"></div> <!-- Blue -->
<div style="background-color: hsl(39, 100%, 50%);"></div> <!-- Orange -->
